Introduction

The LMN package provides several functions to facilitate inference with multivariate regression models of the form \[ {\boldsymbol{Y}}\sim {{\scriptsize \textrm{MN}}}({\boldsymbol{X}}_{\boldsymbol{\theta}}{\boldsymbol{\beta}}, {\boldsymbol{V}}_{\boldsymbol{\theta}}, {\boldsymbol{\Sigma}}), \] where \({\boldsymbol{Y}}_{n\times q}\) is the response matrix, \({\boldsymbol{X}}_{\boldsymbol{\theta}}= {\boldsymbol{X}}_{n \times p}\) is the design matrix which depends on parameters \({\boldsymbol{\theta}}\), \({\boldsymbol{V}}_{\boldsymbol{\theta}}= {\boldsymbol{V}}_{n \times n}\) and \({\boldsymbol{\Sigma}}_{q \times q}\) are between-row and between-column variance matrices, \({\boldsymbol{\beta}}_{p \times q}\) are regression coefficients, and \({{\scriptsize \textrm{MN}}}({\boldsymbol{\Lambda}}, {\boldsymbol{\Omega}}, {\boldsymbol{\Sigma}})\) denotes the Matrix-Normal distribution: \[ {\boldsymbol{Z}}_{p \times q} \sim {{\scriptsize \textrm{MN}}}({\boldsymbol{\Lambda}}_{p \times q}, {\boldsymbol{\Omega}}_{p \times p}, {\boldsymbol{\Sigma}}_{q \times q}) \quad \iff \quad {{\small \textrm{vec}}}({\boldsymbol{Z}}) \sim {\mathcal{N}}({{\small \textrm{vec}}}({\boldsymbol{\Lambda}}), {\boldsymbol{\Sigma}}\otimes {\boldsymbol{\Omega}}). \] The parameters of the regression model are \({\boldsymbol{\Theta}}= ({\boldsymbol{\theta}}, {\boldsymbol{\beta}}, {\boldsymbol{\Sigma}})\).

Example 2: Generalized Cox-Ingersoll-Ross Process
